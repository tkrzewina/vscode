How it works

Being mindful and looking after your mind can be easier than you think. We have been doing it for a long time as children and it's finally time to bring it back.

  • Jigsaw puzzles are a great tool for mindfulness

    Set up your jigsaw puzzle work station somewhere quiet where you can focus on it completely. When you start getting distracted, just notice that it is happening and refocus on the puzzle.

  • Take it step by step

    Try to work on it in 10-20 minute sessions at the time and give it your undevided attention. Don't be overwhelmed by the amount of pieces. Start with finding the corners, then the edges, then look for sections of similar shade or colour.

  • Find the easter eggs

    Our jigsaws have hidden objects on them so the actual jigsaw puzzle is slightly different than what you see on the image. Once you find an easter egg, its a good time to stop your session. Give yourself one easter egg per session.

  • Unwind

    Always remember to relax! This is time for yourself when you stop worrying about things and just enjoy the present moment.
